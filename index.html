What has been found so far

1. A strong medieval surname corridor

Rokele / de la Rochelle / Rupella records appear in Anglo-Norman England and Ireland, including Justiciar Sir Richard de Rupella in Ireland from 1245-46 and Iverk/Kilkenny free-tenant evidence in 1314.

2. South-east Ireland continuity signals

The strongest local corridor runs through Iverk, Fiddown/Kelroske, Waterford, Carrick/Suir, Gortardagh/Rockett's Castle, and Ballytarsna. The 1314->1590s and 1635->1720 generation bridges remain open.

3. The Norman-origin question is nuanced

Direct d'Eu honour land transmission is not supported, but de Ou/Doe, Brinson/de Brienchun, and Mandeville witness-network evidence means the broader d'Eu/Rokele interaction question should not be treated as monolithically closed.

4. DNA testing is the next independent anchor

Y-DNA and mtDNA tests have been ordered. Y-DNA is the relevant test for the direct Rockett paternal line; mtDNA follows a separate direct maternal line.

What Y-DNA may add

  • Y-DNA testing can anchor the direct Rockett paternal line without dilution, but it cannot label a branch as Norman by itself.
  • The strongest future test is comparison with other male-line Rockett/Rocket/Rokell/Rockell/Rochwell testers.
  • mtDNA follows a separate direct maternal line and is useful for personal ancestry, but it will not test the Rockett surname line unless the surname sits on that maternal chain.

Open questions

  • Bridge the 1314 Iverk Rockett/Rokell evidence to the 1590s-1630s Waterford Rockett records with named generations.
  • Bridge Edward/Maria Rockall c.1635 to Petrus Rocket c.1720 Ballytarsna.
  • Resolve Athloypot/Atlongport at townland or sub-denomination level from original NAI/TCD material.
  • Test the d'Eu/Brinson connection only through targeted primary sources, not broad re-sweeps.
  • Use Y-DNA results to see whether the Drumcannon/Ballytarsna Rockett paternal line clusters with other south-east Ireland Rockett lines.
